About Me

I’m a cybersecurity and IT professional in my thirties with a hands-on background in technical support, logistics, and systems operations, now transitioning fully into cybersecurity and SOC-level work.

I grew up in Michigan and experienced firsthand how quickly financial stability can change during the housing market crash. That period shaped my understanding of resilience and long-term planning, especially while navigating housing instability and supporting family responsibilities.

My early leadership experience began in JROTC, where I served as a platoon leader and developed discipline, structure, and accountability—skills that later carried into both my civilian and technical roles.

My professional path has taken me through a wide range of environments, from industrial logistics and production environments to technical and customer-facing roles. I’ve worked in food production environments, appliance delivery for Best Buy, and customer support roles handling sensitive systems and information.

A turning point in my career came after physically demanding roles involving heavy lifting and fast-paced operational work. I transitioned toward IT and cybersecurity while continuing to support my family at home.

I am currently completing my Master’s degree in Cybersecurity at DeVry University, with only a few classes remaining before graduation. I’ve built hands-on experience in Active Directory, virtualization, network defense, and security monitoring through labs and personal projects.

Today, my focus is on breaking into a SOC Analyst or cybersecurity operations role where I can apply real-world technical experience, problem-solving ability, and persistence in a security-focused environment.
